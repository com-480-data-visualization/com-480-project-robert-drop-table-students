Tinkering with the graph shows that newer talktalks tend to have shorter durations, slower speed of speech, fewer number of comments and views.

Indeed, the speed of speech has decreased over the years, though this may not be caused by the general decadence, but rather the usage of videos and music in modern TEDs together with better speaker skills may keep the speed in check.

Speed of speech also apparently gives some boost to the number of comments and the number of views of a talk: Better to talk reallyreally fast so that all the people will find you more engaging!

To enhance the visualization we removed rows with duration under 30 minutes and film date over 2001. Speed of speech is zero if the given talk lacks the transcript.
